Naval Aircraft at Public Events

Our office handles all initial requests for aircraft at public events to determine if the Navy can legally provide support.

To determine if your event is eligable you will need to follow the directions below and complete a Request for Military Aerial Support (DD Form 2535).

Please Note:Limited number of aerial demonstrations for FY15

The Department of Defense's FY15 community outreach planning guidance permits the Navy to support a limited number of military aerial demonstrations as put forth in the administrative message here.Every request will be evaluated and approved on a case-by-case basis. Our office reviews the request to determine eligablity (legally supportable) and the Navy Chief of Information (CHINFO) makes the final authorizing decision.

Please do not contact Naval aviation units about your event until event authorization is confirmed.

Flyovers

All flyover requests will be reviewed for an eligibility determination in accordanance with established criteria as set forth in Department of Defense and Navy regulations.

Due to the current fiscal environment and limited number of flyovers allowed we will also evaluate the request while considering things such as: audience reach and size, media coverage potential, recruiting priorities, Gallup data, Chief of Naval Personnel diversity priority markets, previous naval aviation support and flag/general officer, congressional, or other high-level interest.

Please follow the directionsbelow to submit a request.

Static Displays

The Navy is permitted to fly to 10 civilian air shows for static display support in FY15.

Additionally, a squadron can support static display support in conjunction with mission-related travel. Squadrons conducting training all over the country and can stop at an event if it is en-route. Pending squadron There is no limit to the amount of the event

We maintain a list of air shows authorized for static display support as well as a list of authorized events coinciding with mission related-travel: 2015 Aviation Events.

Please follow the directionsbelow to submit a request.

Submit a Request

Four requirments must be met for a request to be fullfilled.

  • Site certification from the FAA
  • Deemed eligabile by NAVCO (our office)
  • Authorization by the Navy Chief of Information (CHINFO)
  • Commitment from a Navy Squadron to support request

1. Fill out a DD Form 2535.

Download Request for Military Aerial Support (DD Form 2535)

Ink signatures required in:

Box 3d Event Site Certification, signed by an agent exercising authority for site use at requested location.

Box 25a President/Chairman, signed by the senior official (president, chairman, commander, etc.) of requesting organization.

If you are the authorizing authority for the site use and the senior official of the requesting organization, sign both 3d and 25a.

2. Send DD Form 2535 to local FAA FSDO

An FAA Flight Standards District Officer (FSDO) inspector must fill out section IV and sign box 19d. Search for your regional FSDO inspectors here.

Ink signatures required in:

Box 19d: FAA Coordinating Official, signed by the FSDO in the region of the requested flyover.

3. Scan and Send Form to Navy

Scan and send a .pdf of the form to .

Please specify whether it is an air-show, flyover or static display/fly-in request in the subject line.

4. Eligable/non-eligable status determinitation

Non-eligable event: if the event is deemed non-eligable we provide you with an explination of the policy that prevents the Navy from fullfilling your request.

Eligable event: sent to the Navy Chief of Information (CHINFO) for authorization decision.

Please note that an eligible status does not a guarantee support. The request still needs to be authorized and the aviation units in your area may have operational obligations or previously scheduled events that could prevent support.

5. Authorization from CHINFO

We will notify you once a decision is made.

If given authorization you may begin contacting naval aviation units in your area directly to find a squadron that can fullfill the request.

Squadrons will do everything they can to support your request but please keep in mind that operational obligations or previously scheduled events may prevent them from providing support.

6. Find aircraft

Contact and coordination must be done by the requestor. Our office cannot provide coordination support.

Search our map of all airfields with naval aircraft here.

Contact the aviation coordinators listed on the map to find a unit that can support the request.

The Navy Office of Community Outreach does not own aircraft and cannot fullfill the actual request. We can only determine if an event is eligable and seek authorization. Coordination is handled directly between Navy aviation activities and event orginizers.
